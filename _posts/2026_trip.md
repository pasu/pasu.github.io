## ☀️ The Clear Day: Alpine Adventure in Garmisch-Partenkirchen

If the forecast shows clear skies and high visibility, head straight to the mountains. This is your chance to visit the **Zugspitze** (Germany's highest peak at $2962\text{ m}$) or the stunning **Partnach Gorge**.

1. **Morning Train from Munich:** ~1h 20m.
Take a direct regional train (RB) from München Hbf to Garmisch-Partenkirchen. They run roughly every hour.


2. **Summit the Zugspitze:** Morning.
Right behind the Garmisch mainline station, hop onto the historic **Bayerische Zugspitzbahn** (cogwheel train). It takes you scenicly up to the glacier plateau (*Zugspitzplatt*). From there, take the short Gletscherbahn cable car to the absolute summit for 360-degree views across four countries.


3. **Descend via Eibsee:** Early Afternoon.
Take the spectacular **Cable Car Zugspitze** down from the summit directly to **Eibsee**, a crystal-clear, emerald-green alpine lake at the base of the mountain. Walk along the lakeside path to relax.


4. **Explore Partnach Gorge or Town:** Late Afternoon.
Take the cogwheel train back down to Garmisch. If you still have energy, walk through the **Partnachklamm (Partnach Gorge)** to experience roaring waterfalls between massive limestone walls. Otherwise, stroll through the town center of Partenkirchen to see the traditional painted houses (*Lüftlmalerei*).

## ☁️ / 🌧️ The Overcast or Rainy Day: Munich City Culture

If the mountains are trapped in heavy clouds or rain, stay in Munich. The city is packed with world-class museums, indoor food markets, and massive historic halls that are completely weatherproof.

* **Morning:** Head to the **Viktualienmarkt**, a historic open-air food market. Grab a classic Bavarian breakfast of *Weißwurst* (white veal sausage) and soft pretzels. If it drizzles, duck into the massive, neighboring **Frauenkirche** or the grand **Residenz** (the sprawling palace of the Bavarian monarchs).
* **Afternoon:** If you and your son love technology and engineering, spend the afternoon at the **Deutsches Museum**. Located on an island in the Isar River, it is the world's largest museum of science and technology. It features massive interactive halls dedicated to aviation, aerospace, marine navigation, and physics—perfect for a teenager or curious mind on a rainy day.
* **Evening:** Wind down with dinner in a historic beer hall like the **Hofbräuhaus** or the more local **Augustiner-Keller** for a hearty, warm Bavarian meal to escape the damp weather.

---

> **Ticketing Tip for Garmisch:** Look into the **Guten Tag Ticket** (offered by the BRB train line) or the **Bayern Ticket** (Bavaria Ticket) for your transit. They offer incredibly cheap, unlimited regional train travel for a parent and child for the day, which keeps your plans flexible since you don't have to lock in a specific train time in advance.
